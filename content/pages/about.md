# Open/Project Tz’ib  

Open/Project Tz’ib is an open initiative that integrates archaeology and engineering to develop technological solutions for field research, documentation, and knowledge dissemination. The project operates within the context of active archaeological efforts, aiming to modernize workflows through accessible, scalable, and open-source tools.

“Tz’ib,” meaning *writing* or *record* in Mayan languages, reflects our commitment to documenting, preserving, and sharing cultural heritage through technology.

## The Challenge

Archaeological work in the field faces several operational and technological limitations:

- Difficulty in terrain mapping and analysis (LiDAR accessibility)  
- Lack of efficient tools for field documentation of findings  
- Limited integration between data capture, storage, and analysis  

## Our projects

### Field Technology
- Mobile-first platform (**Kubemaya**) for real-time documentation  
  - Automatic GPS capture  
  - Photo upload  
  - Audio recording + AI transcription  
  - Preliminary structured descriptions  

### Spatial & Imaging Technologies
- LiDAR data processing and accessibility  
- Drone-based photogrammetry  
- Experimental mobile mapping (vehicle-based scanning)

### Knowledge Sharing
- Internal blog platform for archaeologists  
- Open documentation and research notes  
- Public-facing content for knowledge dissemination

## Our Approach

We believe in:

- Open collaboration between disciplines  
- Accessible technology for field environments
